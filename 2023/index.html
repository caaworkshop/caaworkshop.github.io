Introduction

The 2nd International Cryptoasset Analytics Workshop (CAAW) brings together researchers from different academic disciplines to present their newest findings related to cryptoassets and their ecosystems; learn about novel analytics methods spanning all layers (P2P network, consensus, on-chain, off-chain); and discuss open challenges and possible future directions. Since cryptoasset analytics has become relevant across disciplines, we envision this workshop as an interdisciplinary venue, also connecting to cross-cutting issues related to law, ethics, privacy, and security.

The program of CAAW 2023 workshop (co-located with TheWebConf2023) features a mix of invited talks and a selection of peer-reviewed research contributions. Workshop topics range from empirical studies, over analytics methods and tools, to case studies, datasets, and cross-cutting issues like legal or ethical aspects. Scientists and professionals from all disciplines and sectors are invited to share their knowledge and experience in this niche intersection of Cryptoassets and Analytics through this workshop, as well as in the future iterations of CAAW.

Workshop Program

CAAW 2023 is a half-day workshop — 1 May 2023, 9:00 am - 12:30 pm (CDT) — co-located with ACM TheWebConf2023.

Session 1

1 May 2023     |     9:00 am - 10:30 pm (CDT)

Session Chair : Jiahua Xu

  • 9:00 am - 9:10 am : Introduction to the Workshop by the Program Co-Chairs
  • 9:10 am - 9:45 am : Invited Talk — Dominant currencies in Decentralized Finance (DeFi), by Kathy Yuan [view abstract]
    In this keynote speech, we explore the rapidly evolving world of decentralized finance (DeFi) to understand the making of dominant currencies and extract valuable insights for the broader financial ecosystem. By examining data from the Ethereum blockchain, we identify patterns and attributes contributing to a currency's dominance, such as active money markets, market size, and correlation with transaction costs. These findings can inform essential design choices for future central bank digital currencies (CBDCs) and shed light on the complex dynamics of currency dominance in an increasingly decentralized financial landscape.
  • 9:45 am - 10:05 pm : Paper Presentation — An Exploration on Cryptocurrency Corporations' Fiscal Opportunities, by Thomas Charest and Masarah Paquet-Clouston.
  • 10:05 pm - 10:25 pm : Paper Presentation — Regime-based Implied Stochastic Volatility Model for Crypto Option Pricing, by Danial Saef, Yuanrong Wang and Tomaso Aste.
  • 12:25 pm - 12:30 pm : Poster Presentation — An Approach to Systematically Assess the Solvency of VASPs: An Austrian Case Study, by Pietro Saggese, Burkhard Raunig, Esther Segalla, Michael Sigmund, Felix Zangerl, Stefan Kitzler and Bernhard Haslhofer

Session 2

1 May 2023     |     11:00 am - 12:30 pm (CDT)

Session Chair : Friedhelm Victor

  • 11:00 am - 11:40 am : Invited Talk — From Theory to Practice: Tracing Pig Butchers, by Rohan Aggarwal [view abstract]
    Pig butchering scams in cryptocurrency involve unscrupulous actors who manipulate and deceive investors through a calculated strategy of trust-building and persuasion. These scams ultimately lead to the theft of digital assets. In this talk, we outline and quantify the phenomena, and illustrate how stolen funds can be traced through cross-chain swap services.
  • 11:40 am - 12:00 pm : Paper Presentation — Ready, Aim, Snipe! Analysis of Sniper Bots and their Impact on the DeFi Ecosystem, by Federico Cernera, Massimo La Morgia, Alessandro Mei, Alberto Maria Mongardini and Francesco Sassi.
  • 12:00 pm - 12:20 pm : Paper Presentation — Don't Trust, Verify: The Case of Slashing from a Popular Ethereum Explorer, by Zhiguo He, Jiasun Li and Zhengxun Wu.
  • 12:20 pm - 12:30 pm : Concluding remarks by the Organizers
